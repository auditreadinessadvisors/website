Internal Controls & Financial Reporting Advisory

Build, evaluate, and strengthen the internal control environment your auditor expects — and your stakeholders require.

The Problem

Weak or undocumented internal controls are one of the most common reasons audits go sideways. Control deficiencies lead to audit findings, Management Letter comments, and in severe cases, qualified audit opinions that undermine investor and lender confidence.

Many companies — especially fast-growing startups, recently acquired entities, and organizations expanding into new markets — have outgrown their control environment. Processes that worked at a smaller scale now create risk at the current level of complexity.

How We Help

We bring Big Four-level internal controls expertise to design, evaluate, and strengthen your control environment:

Controls Assessment & Design

  • End-to-end process mapping for key financial reporting cycles
  • Risk identification and control point mapping
  • Control design recommendations aligned with COSO framework
  • Policy and procedure documentation
  • Segregation of duties analysis

Controls Testing & Evaluation

  • Design and operating effectiveness testing
  • Walkthrough testing of key controls
  • IT general controls (ITGC) assessment
  • Deficiency classification (significant deficiency vs. material weakness)
  • Gap analysis against auditor expectations

Financial Reporting Advisory

  • Financial close process optimization
  • Management reporting and KPI alignment with external reporting
  • Consolidation process design for multi-entity groups
  • Intercompany transaction controls and eliminations
  • Board reporting package development

Who This Is For

  • Companies that received control deficiency findings from prior audits
  • Fast-growing organizations whose controls haven't scaled with the business
  • Companies preparing for SOX compliance or SOC reporting
  • PE-backed companies implementing governance frameworks post-acquisition
  • Multinational entities needing consistent controls across subsidiaries
  • Startups building their financial reporting infrastructure from the ground up
