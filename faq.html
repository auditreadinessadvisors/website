Audit Readiness & Mock Audits

An audit readiness review is a comprehensive pre-audit assessment that evaluates your financial statements, supporting documentation, internal controls, and accounting policies against the standards your auditor will apply. It identifies potential audit adjustments, documentation gaps, and compliance issues before the formal audit begins — giving you time to remediate and present clean financials.
A mock audit simulates the full audit experience — including document requests, walkthroughs, testing procedures, and management inquiries — without the formal opinion or regulatory consequences. It's a risk-free rehearsal that exposes weaknesses and prepares your team for the real thing. Unlike a statutory audit, a mock audit is advisory in nature and designed to help you improve.
Any organization preparing for a financial audit benefits from audit readiness services. This is especially critical for venture-backed startups facing their first audit, companies transitioning between accounting frameworks (e.g., local GAAP to IFRS), organizations with complex structures like holding companies or multinational groups, and businesses that have experienced significant growth, M&A activity, or changes in their financial reporting requirements.
Most audit readiness reviews are completed within 3–6 weeks, depending on the size and complexity of your organization. We recommend starting at least 8–12 weeks before your planned audit date to allow adequate time for remediation. Timelines can be accelerated for urgent engagements.

Technical Accounting & Frameworks

We provide advisory services across US GAAP, IFRS, and NIIF (the Spanish-language implementation of IFRS used in Colombia and other Latin American countries). Our team has deep cross-framework expertise for companies operating across multiple jurisdictions.
US GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles) is the framework required for US public companies and widely used by private companies in the United States. IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards) is the global framework used in most countries outside the US. NIIF (Normas Internacionales de Información Financiera) is the Spanish-language equivalent of IFRS, adopted in Colombia and other Latin American countries. While both share common principles, they diverge in areas like revenue recognition, lease accounting, and financial instruments.
NIIF (Normas Internacionales de Información Financiera) is the Colombian adoption of International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). Since 2015, most Colombian companies are required to prepare their financial statements under NIIF. Proper NIIF compliance is essential for regulatory filings with the Superintendencia de Sociedades and for attracting international investors or partners.
